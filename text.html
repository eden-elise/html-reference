Vector Spaces

1. Definitions and Examples

A vector space is a set V over a field F together with two operations: addition and scalar multiplication. The field F is typically the real numbers ℝ or the complex numbers ℂ.

The study of vector spaces is central to linear algebra, a branch of mathematics with applications in computer graphics, quantum mechanics, and machine learning.

1.1 The Definition of a Vector Space

The definition of a vector space is the foundation upon which all of linear algebra is built. Understanding it deeply is more valuable than memorizing theorems.

— Linear Algebra Done Right, Sheldon Axler

As Axler notes, a list of length zero is a basis of the zero vector space — a degenerate case worth remembering.
