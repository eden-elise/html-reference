Text Structure

p for paragraphs

The <p> element is the basic unit of prose in HTML.

Multiple paragraphs create separation between distinct thoughts.

blockquote for block-level quotes

The blockquote element represents content that is quoted from another source.

— W3C HTML Specification

q for inline quotes

The spec also says the cite attribute may be used to link to the source.

pre for preformatted text 

            f(x) = x^2 + 2x + 1
            g(x) = (x + 1)^2
            therefore: f(x) = g(x)

hr for a thematic break

The section above and below are thematically distinct, separated by an hr:

New topic begins here.

br for line breaks

Emphasis & Meaning

em — stress emphasis

I never said she stole the money.

strong — importance

Warning: do not close the browser during submission.

mark — highlighted/relevant text

The most important axiom is closure under scalar multiplication.

small — fine print or side comments

Price: $9.99 (excludes tax)

s — no longer accurate

The exam is on Friday on Monday.

del and ins — tracked deletion and insertion

The passing grade is 70% 75%.

b — stylistically bold, no semantic importance, not good practice

The keywords are array, loop, and function.

i — stylistically italic, e.g. technical terms, not good practice

The term eigenvalue comes from the German Eigenwert.

u — underline, valid for proper nouns in some contexts

The name Euler appears throughout mathematics.
